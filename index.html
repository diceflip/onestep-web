Frequently Asked Questions

There are some questions we get asked frequently.

1. What is OneStep?

OneStep is a community-based platform that connects individuals, restaurants, and stores with local shelters and people in need to share surplus food. Our goal is to reduce food waste, combat food insecurity, and build a stronger community.

2. How does it work?

Donors list their surplus food items on our platform. Recipients browse the listings and request what they need. We connect donors and recipients to arrange pickup or delivery.

3. Is it free?

Yes, OneStep is completely free for both donors and recipients.

4. What types of food can be donated?

You can donate a wide variety of food items, including fresh produce, packaged goods, canned goods, baked goods, and prepared meals (with appropriate handling guidelines). Please ensure all donated food is safe for consumption and within its expiration date.

5. How do I know the food is safe?

We provide guidelines on safe food handling and storage practices for donors. We encourage recipients to inspect all donated food before consumption.
